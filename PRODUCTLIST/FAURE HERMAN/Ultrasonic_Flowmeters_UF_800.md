## Detailed Description

### Overview
Ultrasonic flow measurement offers distinct advantages in modern piping systems—namely, zero pressure drop, no moving parts, and bi-directional capability. The **Faure Herman UF 800** series leverages multi-path transit-time ultrasonic technology to deliver precise volumetric flow rates, even in disturbed velocity profiles or changing fluid properties. 

### Multi-Chord Transit-Time Technology
The UF 800 works by sending ultrasonic pulses back and forth diagonally across the pipe. Pulses traveling with the flow move faster than pulses traveling against it. By measuring this transit-time difference across up to 8 distinct chords, the flow computer integrates the velocity profile across the entire pipe cross-section, ensuring custody-transfer grade accuracy.

### Versatile Transducer Configurations
- **External Clamp-On:** Transducers are mounted onto the outside of the pipe. Best for retrofitting or temporary audits with zero line stoppage.
- **Wetted Insertion:** Transducers are inserted directly into wetted ports. Provides higher accuracy and handles more challenging fluids.
- **Open Channel:** Measures water level and velocity in open conduits and channels.

## Key Features & Benefits
*   **High Repeatability & Accuracy:** Multi-path chord designs minimize sensitivity to piping geometry and upstream disturbances.
*   **No Pressure Drop:** Wetted sensors are flush-mounted, and clamp-on sensors have zero contact, reducing pump power costs.
*   **Portable Auditing:** Portable units include robust cases and rechargeable batteries, ideal for field verification of other meters.
*   **Advanced Diagnostics:** Built-in oscilloscope and signal quality metrics allow operators to verify meter health in real-time.
